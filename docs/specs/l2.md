# L2 — Detailed Requirements

This document defines detailed requirements for QuorumQ. Each requirement has a
unique identifier (`L2-xx`), traces back to one or more L1 requirements in
`l1.md`, and includes detailed Gherkin-style acceptance criteria.

Every L2 requirement is intended to be picked up during implementation using
Acceptance Test Driven Development (ATDD): the acceptance criteria are
translated into failing Playwright end-to-end tests before any production code
is written.

---

## Team Membership

### L2-01 — Create a Team
**Traces to:** L1-01, L1-16

**Description:** An authenticated user shall be able to create a new team,
giving it a name and optional description. The creator automatically becomes
the team owner.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** an authenticated user on the home screen
  **When** they tap "Create Team" and submit a team name of 3–50 characters
  **Then** the team is created and they are redirected to the team dashboard
  as the team owner.
- **Given** an authenticated user
  **When** they submit a team name shorter than 3 characters or longer than 50
  **Then** an inline validation message appears and the team is not created.
- **Given** an authenticated user who just created a team
  **Then** they are listed as the sole team member with the role "Owner".

### L2-02 — Invite Members to a Team
**Traces to:** L1-01, L1-16

**Description:** A team owner or admin shall be able to invite new members via
a shareable invite link or email address.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a team owner on the team settings screen
  **When** they tap "Invite" and generate an invite link
  **Then** a link with an expiring token is displayed and can be copied.
- **Given** a valid invite link
  **When** an authenticated non-member opens it
  **Then** they are prompted to join and, upon accepting, become a team member
  with the role "Member".
- **Given** an expired or revoked invite link
  **When** it is opened
  **Then** the app shows "Invite no longer valid" and offers a contact action.

### L2-03 — Join Multiple Teams
**Traces to:** L1-01

**Description:** A user shall be able to belong to multiple teams and switch
between them.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a user who is a member of two or more teams
  **When** they open the team switcher
  **Then** all their teams are listed and selecting one switches the active
  team context throughout the app.
- **Given** a user with no teams
  **When** they open the app after signing in
  **Then** they are prompted either to create a team or accept an invite.

## User Authentication

### L2-04 — Sign Up with Email
**Traces to:** L1-02, L1-16

**Description:** A new user shall be able to create an account using an email
address and password (or OAuth where supported).

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a visitor on the sign-up screen
  **When** they submit a valid email and a password meeting complexity rules
  **Then** an account is created and a verification email is sent.
- **Given** an unverified user
  **When** they attempt to create a team or vote
  **Then** they are prompted to verify their email first.
- **Given** a visitor submitting a weak password
  **Then** a password strength indicator and inline rules prevent submission.

### L2-05 — Sign In and Sign Out
**Traces to:** L1-02

**Description:** A returning user shall be able to sign in with their
credentials and sign out from any screen.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a registered user
  **When** they submit valid credentials
  **Then** they are signed in and routed to their last active team.
- **Given** a signed-in user
  **When** they tap "Sign out" in the account menu
  **Then** their session is cleared and they return to the landing screen.
- **Given** a user entering invalid credentials three times in a row
  **Then** the app temporarily rate-limits further attempts and displays a
  clear message.

### L2-06 — Session Persistence
**Traces to:** L1-02, L1-16

**Description:** The app shall keep a user signed in across page reloads and
browser restarts until explicit sign-out or session expiry.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a signed-in user
  **When** they close and reopen the browser within the session lifetime
  **Then** they remain signed in.
- **Given** a session that has expired
  **When** the user performs an authenticated action
  **Then** they are prompted to sign in again and, after doing so, resume where
  they left off.

## Lunch Sessions

### L2-07 — Start a Lunch Session
**Traces to:** L1-04, L1-07

**Description:** Any team member shall be able to start a new lunch session for
today, with a configurable voting deadline.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a team member on the team dashboard
  **When** they tap "Start lunch" and confirm a deadline between 5 and 180
  minutes from now
  **Then** a new active lunch session is created and visible to all team
  members immediately.
- **Given** an active lunch session already exists for the team today
  **When** a member taps "Start lunch"
  **Then** they are taken to the existing session instead of creating a
  duplicate.

### L2-08 — Lunch Session States
**Traces to:** L1-04, L1-05

**Description:** A lunch session shall progress through explicit states:
`Suggesting`, `Voting`, `Decided`, and `Cancelled`.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** an active session in `Suggesting`
  **When** the organizer taps "Start voting"
  **Then** the session transitions to `Voting` and new suggestions are locked.
- **Given** a session in `Voting`
  **When** the deadline passes
  **Then** the session automatically transitions to `Decided` and the winner
  is announced.
- **Given** a session in any active state
  **When** the organizer cancels it
  **Then** the session transitions to `Cancelled` and is read-only thereafter.

### L2-09 — View Active Session
**Traces to:** L1-04, L1-07, L1-08

**Description:** The team dashboard shall surface the active lunch session
prominently on mobile.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a team with an active session
  **When** a member opens the dashboard on a phone
  **Then** a full-width card with the session status and a countdown is the
  first element above the fold.
- **Given** a team without an active session
  **Then** the dashboard shows a "Start lunch" primary action at the top.

## Restaurant Suggestions

### L2-10 — Suggest a Restaurant
**Traces to:** L1-03

**Description:** During the `Suggesting` state, any team member shall be able
to suggest a restaurant by name, address, cuisine, and optional link.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a session in `Suggesting`
  **When** a member submits a restaurant with a name of 2–80 characters
  **Then** the restaurant appears in the suggestion list for all members in
  real time.
- **Given** a duplicate restaurant name already suggested in the current
  session
  **When** a member attempts to submit it
  **Then** the app shows "Already suggested by <name>" and offers to upvote the
  existing suggestion instead.
- **Given** a session not in `Suggesting`
  **When** a user tries to submit a suggestion
  **Then** the submission form is disabled with an explanation.

### L2-11 — Reuse Past Restaurants
**Traces to:** L1-03, L1-14

**Description:** When suggesting, the user shall see a search field that
autocompletes from restaurants previously suggested by the team.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a team with at least one historical restaurant
  **When** a user types 2 or more characters in the suggestion field
  **Then** matching restaurants appear as suggestions within 300 ms.
- **Given** a user selects an autocompleted entry
  **Then** the name, cuisine, and address fields are prefilled.

### L2-12 — Remove Own Suggestion
**Traces to:** L1-03

**Description:** A user shall be able to withdraw their own suggestion while
the session is in `Suggesting`.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a user who has suggested a restaurant in the current session
  **When** they tap "Withdraw" on their suggestion
  **Then** the suggestion is removed for all members immediately.
- **Given** the session has moved to `Voting`
  **Then** the "Withdraw" action is no longer available.

## Voting

### L2-13 — Cast a Vote
**Traces to:** L1-05, L1-07

**Description:** During the `Voting` state, each member shall be able to cast
exactly one vote for one suggestion.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a session in `Voting`
  **When** a member taps "Vote" on a suggestion
  **Then** the vote is recorded and the suggestion's vote count updates for
  all members in real time.
- **Given** a member who has already voted
  **When** they tap "Vote" on a different suggestion
  **Then** their vote moves to the new suggestion and the previous count
  decrements.
- **Given** a member who has voted
  **When** they tap their current vote again
  **Then** the vote is cleared.

### L2-14 — Tie Breaking
**Traces to:** L1-05

**Description:** When voting ends with a tie for first place, the system shall
run a visible tie-breaker round limited to tied suggestions and lasting a
default of 2 minutes.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** voting ends with two or more suggestions tied for first
  **Then** a tie-breaker round starts and only tied suggestions are votable.
- **Given** the tie-breaker round ends with a single leader
  **Then** that suggestion becomes the winner and the session state becomes
  `Decided`.
- **Given** the tie-breaker round ends still tied
  **Then** the system randomly selects a winner from the tied set and
  displays "Winner chosen at random" for transparency.

### L2-15 — Announce the Winner
**Traces to:** L1-05, L1-09, L1-15

**Description:** Upon decision, the app shall present a spectacular winner
announcement with the restaurant details and directions.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a session transitions to `Decided`
  **Then** all members see a full-screen animated reveal of the winning
  restaurant within 2 seconds.
- **Given** the winner reveal
  **Then** primary actions "Get directions" and "Open website" are available
  on the reveal screen.

## Comments and Reviews

### L2-16 — Comment on a Suggestion
**Traces to:** L1-06, L1-07

**Description:** During any active session state, members shall be able to
post comments on any suggestion.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a session in an active state
  **When** a member posts a comment of 1–500 characters on a suggestion
  **Then** the comment appears in the suggestion's thread for all members in
  real time and is attributed to the author.
- **Given** a member editing their own comment within 5 minutes of posting
  **Then** they can save edits and the comment is marked "edited".
- **Given** a member's own comment
  **Then** they may delete it and the UI shows "Comment deleted".

### L2-17 — Review a Restaurant
**Traces to:** L1-06, L1-14

**Description:** After a session is `Decided`, members shall be able to leave
a rating (1–5 stars) and optional written review for the winning restaurant.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a `Decided` session with a winner
  **When** a participating member submits a 1–5 star rating
  **Then** the rating is recorded against the restaurant and aggregated into
  its average rating.
- **Given** a member who already reviewed this visit
  **When** they submit another review for the same visit
  **Then** the new review replaces the previous one.
- **Given** a user who did not participate in the session
  **Then** the review form is not available to them.

### L2-18 — View Restaurant Profile
**Traces to:** L1-06, L1-14

**Description:** Each restaurant shall have a profile showing its details,
aggregated rating, and historical reviews from the team.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a user taps a restaurant name anywhere in the app
  **Then** they are taken to the restaurant profile with details, average
  rating, and a list of past reviews with dates and authors.
- **Given** a restaurant with no reviews
  **Then** the profile shows an empty state encouraging the team to choose it.

## Real-time Collaboration

### L2-19 — Real-time Updates
**Traces to:** L1-07

**Description:** Suggestion additions, votes, state transitions, and comments
shall appear to all team members within 2 seconds without a manual refresh.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** two members viewing the same session on separate devices
  **When** one of them votes, comments, or suggests
  **Then** the other device reflects the change within 2 seconds.
- **Given** a device that loses its real-time connection
  **Then** the UI shows a non-intrusive "Reconnecting…" indicator and resyncs
  state upon reconnection.

### L2-20 — Presence
**Traces to:** L1-07

**Description:** The session screen shall show which team members are
currently viewing the session.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a session screen
  **Then** avatars of currently connected members are visible with a subtle
  "online" indicator.
- **Given** a member closes the session screen
  **Then** their presence indicator is cleared within 30 seconds.

## Mobile-First Responsive UI

### L2-21 — Mobile Layout
**Traces to:** L1-08

**Description:** On viewport widths below 640 px the app shall present a
single-column layout with bottom navigation and thumb-reachable primary
actions.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a viewport width of 375 px
  **Then** primary actions (vote, suggest, comment) are reachable within the
  bottom third of the screen.
- **Given** a viewport width of 375 px
  **Then** no horizontal scrolling is required on any screen.

### L2-22 — Tablet and Desktop Layouts
**Traces to:** L1-08

**Description:** On viewports 640 px and above the app shall expand into a
multi-column layout that uses the additional space gracefully.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a viewport width of 1024 px
  **Then** the suggestions list and comments thread are displayed side-by-side.
- **Given** any viewport width between 320 px and 1920 px
  **Then** all content remains legible and no element is clipped.

### L2-23 — Touch and Pointer Parity
**Traces to:** L1-08, L1-10

**Description:** All interactions shall work equivalently with touch, mouse,
and keyboard.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** any interactive element
  **Then** it is operable via tap, click, and keyboard with visible focus
  indicators.
- **Given** touch targets
  **Then** they are at least 44×44 CSS pixels.

## Spectacular UI Design

### L2-24 — Design System
**Traces to:** L1-09

**Description:** The app shall be built on a documented design system with
tokens for color, typography, spacing, elevation, and motion.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** the component library
  **Then** every screen composes components drawn from the design system with
  no one-off ad-hoc styles.
- **Given** two different screens
  **Then** typography, spacing, and color usage are visibly consistent.

### L2-25 — Motion and Delight
**Traces to:** L1-09

**Description:** Key transitions (winner reveal, vote tally changes, state
transitions) shall use fluid, purposeful animation at 60 fps.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a user with `prefers-reduced-motion` unset
  **When** a vote is cast
  **Then** the tally animates smoothly to its new value.
- **Given** a user with `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`
  **Then** non-essential animations are suppressed.

### L2-26 — Dark and Light Themes
**Traces to:** L1-09, L1-10

**Description:** The app shall provide a polished dark and light theme,
respecting the OS preference by default and remembering the user's override.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** an OS set to dark mode and a new user
  **Then** the app opens in dark mode.
- **Given** a user toggles the theme
  **Then** the choice persists across sessions and devices for that user.

## Accessibility

### L2-27 — WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance
**Traces to:** L1-10

**Description:** All screens shall meet WCAG 2.1 AA for color contrast,
keyboard operability, focus management, and semantic structure.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** any screen
  **Then** text has at least a 4.5:1 contrast ratio against its background.
- **Given** any interactive element
  **Then** it is reachable and operable via keyboard only, with a visible
  focus ring.
- **Given** any screen
  **When** audited with an automated a11y tool (e.g. axe)
  **Then** zero critical or serious violations are reported.

### L2-28 — Screen Reader Support
**Traces to:** L1-10

**Description:** Dynamic changes (new vote counts, new comments, state
transitions) shall be announced to assistive technology.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a screen reader user on the session screen
  **When** a new vote is cast
  **Then** a polite live-region announcement conveys the updated tally.
- **Given** a screen reader user
  **When** the session transitions to `Decided`
  **Then** the winner is announced via an assertive live region.

## Performance

### L2-29 — Initial Load Performance
**Traces to:** L1-11

**Description:** The first contentful paint shall occur within 1.5 seconds on
a mid-tier mobile device over a 4G connection.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a cold load on a simulated Moto G4 over 4G
  **Then** Largest Contentful Paint is under 2.5 s and Time to Interactive is
  under 3.5 s.
- **Given** a production build
  **Then** the initial JS bundle sent to mobile is under 200 KB gzipped.

### L2-30 — Runtime Responsiveness
**Traces to:** L1-11

**Description:** User interactions shall feel instant.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a user taps any primary action
  **Then** visible feedback appears within 100 ms.
- **Given** typical usage
  **Then** no interaction blocks the main thread for more than 50 ms
  (Interaction to Next Paint under budget).

## End-to-End Testing with Playwright

### L2-31 — Playwright Test Suite
**Traces to:** L1-12, L1-13

**Description:** The repository shall contain a Playwright end-to-end test
suite that covers all acceptance criteria in this document.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** the repository at any commit on the main branch
  **When** `npm run test:e2e` is executed
  **Then** Playwright runs and all acceptance tests pass.
- **Given** a new L2 requirement is added
  **Then** at least one Playwright test targeting its acceptance criteria is
  added in the same change.

### L2-32 — Page Object Model
**Traces to:** L1-12

**Description:** Playwright tests shall use the Page Object Model so that
selectors and page-specific interactions live in page classes and test files
contain only business-level steps.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** any Playwright test file
  **Then** it contains no raw CSS/XPath selectors; all selectors live in page
  object classes under `tests/e2e/pages/`.
- **Given** a page object class
  **Then** it exposes intention-revealing methods (e.g. `suggestRestaurant`,
  `castVoteFor`) rather than leaking DOM details to tests.

### L2-33 — Cross-browser and Mobile Emulation
**Traces to:** L1-08, L1-12

**Description:** The E2E suite shall run against Chromium, Firefox, and
WebKit, and include a mobile viewport profile.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** the Playwright config
  **Then** projects exist for Chromium, Firefox, WebKit, and a mobile Chrome
  viewport (e.g. Pixel 5).
- **Given** the CI pipeline
  **Then** all projects are executed on every pull request.

### L2-34 — Deterministic Test Data
**Traces to:** L1-12, L1-13

**Description:** E2E tests shall run against a seeded, isolated environment
so they are deterministic and independent.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a test run
  **Then** each test provisions its own team and users via test fixtures and
  tears them down afterward.
- **Given** parallel test execution
  **Then** no test shares mutable state with another.

## Acceptance Test Driven Development

### L2-35 — ATDD Workflow
**Traces to:** L1-13

**Description:** Every user-visible change shall begin with a failing
acceptance test derived from an L2 acceptance criterion.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a new feature branch
  **Then** the first commit that touches application code is preceded by a
  commit introducing a failing Playwright acceptance test that references the
  L2 identifier.
- **Given** a pull request
  **Then** its description links to the L2 identifiers it implements.

### L2-36 — Traceability
**Traces to:** L1-13

**Description:** Every Playwright test shall be tagged with the L2 identifier
it covers so that traceability from requirement to test to implementation is
mechanical.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** any Playwright test
  **Then** its title contains the L2 identifier it verifies
  (e.g. `[L2-13] member can cast a vote`).
- **Given** a traceability report generated from test titles
  **Then** every L2 requirement has at least one covering test.

## History and Transparency

### L2-37 — Session History
**Traces to:** L1-14

**Description:** Each team shall have a history screen listing past lunch
sessions with their winners, participation, and final tallies.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a team with past sessions
  **When** a member opens "History"
  **Then** sessions are listed newest-first with date, winner, and vote tally.
- **Given** a history item is tapped
  **Then** a read-only view of that session (suggestions, votes, comments) is
  shown.

### L2-38 — Export History
**Traces to:** L1-14

**Description:** A team owner shall be able to export the team's session
history as CSV.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a team owner on the history screen
  **When** they tap "Export CSV"
  **Then** a CSV file containing date, winner, tally, and participants is
  downloaded.

## Notifications

### L2-39 — In-app Notifications
**Traces to:** L1-15

**Description:** Members shall receive in-app notifications for key events:
session started, voting started, 5 minutes remaining, session decided.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a member not currently viewing the session
  **When** a session starts or ends
  **Then** a non-blocking in-app toast appears with a deep link to the
  session.
- **Given** a member on the session screen
  **Then** countdown milestones are surfaced inline rather than as toasts to
  avoid redundancy.

### L2-40 — Notification Preferences
**Traces to:** L1-15

**Description:** Each user shall be able to mute or customize notifications
per team.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a user on the notification settings screen
  **When** they disable notifications for a team
  **Then** they receive no further notifications for that team until
  re-enabled.

## Data Privacy and Security

### L2-41 — Team Data Isolation
**Traces to:** L1-16

**Description:** A user shall only be able to read or write data belonging to
teams they are a member of.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a user who is not a member of a team
  **When** they attempt to access that team's session, suggestions, votes, or
  comments through the UI or API
  **Then** the request is denied with a 403-equivalent response and no data
  is leaked.

### L2-42 — Secure Transport and Storage
**Traces to:** L1-16

**Description:** All traffic shall use HTTPS and user credentials shall be
stored using industry-standard hashing (e.g. Argon2/bcrypt).

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** any HTTP request
  **Then** it is served only over HTTPS with HSTS enabled.
- **Given** the user credentials store
  **Then** no plaintext passwords are persisted.

### L2-43 — Account Deletion
**Traces to:** L1-16

**Description:** A user shall be able to delete their account, which removes
their identifying information from the system.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Given** a signed-in user on the account settings screen
  **When** they confirm "Delete my account"
  **Then** their profile is deleted or anonymized and their past votes and
  comments are anonymized to "former member" within 30 days.
